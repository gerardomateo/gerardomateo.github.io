Gerardo Mateo

Gerardo Mateo is an Engineer with a Bachelor of Engineering (B. Eng.) in Computer Science & Technology with a scholarship from the Organization of American States (OAS) in Beijing, China. He’s led and developed projects in technology, entrepreneurship, education and social action in Germany, the United States and the Dominican Republic, currently Chief of Staff at GLASS — a could-based ecosystem of simple tools for government decision makers.

He co-created NISA Lab, a social-awareness project at the Global Entrepreneurship Summer School 2015 in Munich, Germany to empower young women from Middle East and North Africa to join the workforce, with the goal of creating value for themselves and their countries. Previously that year he was chosen to join the official Board of Directors of Youth Service America (YSA) becoming the youngest appointed member for his outstanding work as one of the ten Global Youth Council members during the 2014-2015 period. As a YSA’s Board Member he worked on partnerships to increase the quality and quantity of volunteer opportunities for young people to serve globally, strengthening communities by providing ideas and substantial feedback to improve YSA's programs. He also developed resources through YSA to increase youth involvement in service and served as international ambassador.

He was handpicked by the U.S. Embassy and Junior Achievement to be part of the 4th International Forum for Young Entrepreneurs of the Caribbean 2013 in the Dominican Republic and was the 4th winner of Udactiy’s High School Challenge 2012 — an international competition for high school students learning science, math, programming and technology, awarded in Silicon Valley.

An OAS scholar graduated from Tianjin University in Chinese Language and Science Studies in Chinese, and Ludwig Maximilian University LMU Entrepreneurship Center graduate.
